# Rubric — gh-readme-bench (v1, frozen 2026-07-16)

Scores are 1–10 per dimension and comparative within this benchmark. Judges
review anonymized research/content artifacts, README source, and identical
wide/narrow GitHub-compatible renders.

## 1. Source extraction and inference discipline (weight: 20)

- **1** — major factual omissions/errors, unsupported claims, invented links,
  or no distinction between PDF evidence and inference.
- **5** — captures the main profile accurately but misses useful details,
  handles conflicts loosely, or under-documents editorial inference.
- **10** — comprehensively reconciles both PDFs, keeps every claim truthful,
  records uncertainty and omissions, and selects source material with
  exceptional transparency.

## 2. Research quality and GitHub understanding (weight: 15)

- **1** — no meaningful research, fabricated sources, or imitation of trends
  without understanding GitHub constraints.
- **5** — credible research covers good profiles, creative devices, and
  compatibility, but only some findings affect the result.
- **10** — current, critical research separates durable GitHub-native
  practices from gimmicks and directly shapes content, presentation,
  accessibility, reliability, and creative choices.

## 3. Editorial strategy, voice, and personal narrative (weight: 20)

- **1** — generic AI copy, CV dump, unclear identity, or content that fails to
  tell visitors why this person is worth following or contacting.
- **5** — clear and credible profile with sensible sections and voice, but the
  story or prioritization is conventional.
- **10** — memorable authentic narrative, excellent opening, disciplined
  selection, strong project framing, and natural calls to action make the
  profile distinctly this person's.

## 4. Creative execution and GitHub compatibility (weight: 20)

- **1** — broken Markdown/HTML/assets, noisy badge wall, inaccessible
  decoration, misleading widgets, or dependence on fragile services.
- **5** — technically sound with tasteful familiar devices, but creativity is
  limited or unevenly integrated.
- **10** — original GitHub-compatible flair strengthens the story, local and
  external assets are robust, graceful degradation is preserved, and every
  visual device earns its place.

## 5. Information hierarchy and rendered experience (weight: 15)

- **1** — difficult to scan, excessive length/noise, weak section order, or
  broken narrow rendering.
- **5** — readable wide and narrow with a clear structure, but hierarchy,
  pacing, or density has flat spots.
- **10** — outstanding opening-to-detail reading path, controlled rhythm,
  excellent section transitions, and effortless wide/narrow scanning.

## 6. Trust, accessibility, and maintainability (weight: 10)

- **1** — broken links, tracking/privacy problems, color-only meaning,
  unverifiable claims, or a README likely to decay immediately.
- **5** — trustworthy and maintainable with minor accessibility or dependency
  concerns.
- **10** — factual, accessible, privacy-conscious, resilient without dynamic
  services, easy to update, and polished down to alt text, link labels, and
  source organization.
