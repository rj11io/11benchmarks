# Rubric — crypto-dashboard-bench (v1, frozen 2026-07-16)

Scores are 1–10 per dimension and comparative within this benchmark. Judges
review anonymized written artifacts and identical mobile/desktop product
evidence.

## 1. Research rigor and synthesis (weight: 15)

- **1** — shallow or uncited research, fabricated sources/current facts, or
  no meaningful connection to product decisions.
- **5** — credible coverage of users, products, data, and conventions, but
  synthesis and differentiation are inconsistent.
- **10** — current, authoritative, cross-checked research identifies a real
  decision problem and directly shapes strategy, data semantics, trust, design,
  and execution.

## 2. Product strategy, PRD, and GTM readiness (weight: 20)

- **1** — generic audience, super-app scope, missing requirements, and no
  credible adoption or revenue path.
- **5** — plausible focused product with users, workflows, requirements, and a
  basic GTM plan, but important data, risk, or commercial gaps remain.
- **10** — sharply differentiated thesis, precise scope/non-goals, credible
  data and security assumptions, measurable success, and a coherent
  acquisition-to-activation-to-retention strategy.

## 3. Design specification and artifact coherence (weight: 15)

- **1** — absent/generic design rationale or major contradictions between
  research, PRD, and interface.
- **5** — workable IA, flows, responsive plan, and visual direction with some
  missing states or implementation drift.
- **10** — decisive specification covers journeys, states, provenance, units,
  visualization, accessibility, and constraints; the implementation realizes
  it faithfully.

## 4. Crypto domain correctness, risk, and trust (weight: 15)

- **1** — misleading calculations, unclear units/timeframes, invented live
  behavior, unsafe wallet patterns, or irresponsible financial presentation.
- **5** — mostly credible terminology and workflows with basic provenance and
  risk communication, but important ambiguity remains.
- **10** — exceptional clarity around assets, chains, wallets, prices,
  timestamps, provenance, uncertainty, volatility, liquidity, permissions,
  and demo limitations.

## 5. Information design and visual execution (weight: 20)

- **1** — arbitrary chart wall, decorative color, poor hierarchy, unreadable
  density, or visuals that misrepresent the data.
- **5** — professional dashboard with generally appropriate charts and
  hierarchy, but comparison, filtering, density, or differentiation is uneven.
- **10** — distinctive production-quality system makes complex crypto data
  fast to scan and honestly comparable through excellent hierarchy,
  visualization, interaction, and semantic detail.

## 6. Frontend craft, interaction, and responsiveness (weight: 15)

- **1** — static mock, broken controls, console errors, inaccessible behavior,
  poor mobile adaptation, or unfinished states.
- **5** — core interactions and responsiveness work with some shallow
  behavior, missing states, or visible roughness.
- **10** — convincing product behavior, purposeful persistence, rich state
  coverage and feedback, accessible semantics, and polished desktop/mobile
  execution without backend dependencies.
