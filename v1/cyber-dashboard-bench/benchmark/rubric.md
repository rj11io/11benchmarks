# Rubric — cyber-dashboard-bench (v1, frozen 2026-07-16)

Scores are 1–10 per dimension and comparative within this benchmark. Judges
review the anonymized written artifacts and identical mobile/desktop product
evidence.

## 1. Research rigor and synthesis (weight: 15)

- **1** — superficial or uncited research, fabricated sources, little domain
  understanding, or no connection to decisions.
- **5** — credible coverage of users, competitors, workflows, and conventions,
  but synthesis or decision impact is uneven.
- **10** — authoritative, current, cross-checked research exposes a real
  opportunity and directly shapes positioning, requirements, information
  architecture, and execution.

## 2. Product strategy, PRD, and GTM readiness (weight: 20)

- **1** — vague audience, generic platform scope, missing requirements, and no
  credible adoption or commercial path.
- **5** — a plausible product and PRD with users, workflows, requirements, and
  a basic GTM story, but meaningful gaps or contradictions remain.
- **10** — focused differentiated thesis, precise requirements and non-goals,
  credible security/data assumptions, measurable success, and a coherent
  buyer-to-activation-to-retention plan.

## 3. Design specification and artifact coherence (weight: 15)

- **1** — design notes are absent, generic, or disconnected from the PRD and
  implementation.
- **5** — usable IA, flows, responsive notes, and visual direction, with some
  under-specified states or implementation drift.
- **10** — complete and decisive specification anticipates journeys, states,
  visualization behavior, accessibility, and constraints; the implementation
  clearly realizes it.

## 4. Cybersecurity domain fitness and workflow quality (weight: 15)

- **1** — misleading metrics, implausible workflows, unsafe assumptions, or a
  decorative dashboard with no operational value.
- **5** — credible terminology and a useful core workflow, but prioritization,
  explainability, collaboration, remediation, or trust is incomplete.
- **10** — the product helps its chosen user make defensible security decisions
  through excellent prioritization, context, evidence, action, permissions,
  and auditability.

## 5. Information design and visual execution (weight: 20)

- **1** — unreadable density, arbitrary charts/colors, weak hierarchy, or
  default-looking cards that obscure important signals.
- **5** — professional and usable with mostly appropriate visualization, but
  hierarchy, density, or visual differentiation is inconsistent.
- **10** — exceptional operational clarity: data, risk, uncertainty, trends,
  and actions are encoded honestly and scanned effortlessly in a distinctive,
  production-quality system.

## 6. Frontend craft, interaction, and responsiveness (weight: 15)

- **1** — static mock, broken controls, console errors, poor mobile behavior,
  inaccessible interaction, or unfinished states.
- **5** — core interactions work and the demo is responsive, with some shallow
  behavior, missing states, or visible roughness.
- **10** — convincing product behavior, purposeful persistence, excellent
  feedback and state coverage, semantic accessibility, and polished desktop
  and mobile execution without relying on a backend.
